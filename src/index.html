What is web-accessibility?

Accessibility on the internet refers to the practice of making websites accessible to people with disabilities. This can be achieved in a variety of ways, but such standards are well outlined in the W3C's Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1

How is web-accessibility achieved?

Good web-accessibility is achieved by examining problem areas in terms of webpage design and considering where improvements can be made. Common areas of concern include webpage responsiveness, font size and color, as well as screen reader support.

What makes a website accessible?

Websites that adhere to good web-accessibility standards, that is, websites that present their content clearly (with accesible fonts/colors), as well as responsiveness in site design provide a much better user experience for those with or without disabilities.

Types of disabilities to consider...

There are a few core disabilities that have to be accounted for when designing webpages. Some of which include color blindness and other visual disabilities (including total blindness), as well as auditory disabilities.

From a web developer's standpoint

When web developers approach the topic of accessibility, they must be prepared to incorporate the necessary guidlines for making sites more accessible. Some of the things they can do to achieve this standard is by making web pages include ARIA support (for screen readers), as well as designing pages to follow accessibility standards (fonts/colors/text sizes etc.) and responsive design.
